Chapter 2

An Apple a Day

Can a machine empathize with humans?

Enormous composite polymer gates lift of the ground in a raucous rumble. Beyond the gate is a wide, sand paved road that eventually diffuses into the forest. On the sides are a human settlement with one-story houses and buildings each made out of either wood, mud, stones, or crude bricks. Directly above the road, spanning the whole width, are various bridges hoisted up by the trees; There are people walking across. The various men and women on the ground, only seconds ago caught in conversation, are now silent as the convoy of robots make their way through the gate. There are no guards in sight, but the humans know all too well that the robots do not like to show off their highly advanced weaponry for humans to imitate; The guards are hidden, but always ready to strike. As such, no humans run through the closing gates, nor do they so much as approach the robots. Within this convoy are two diplomats, and two human doctors. In their arms they carry various crates of supplies with ease. However, they seem to be carrying less than these convoys usually carry. This ritual, whilst always a spectacle to behold, is most certainly expected by the humans of this settlement as this convoy sets out every week. A strict protocol was negotiated, and the robots proceed with the plan as they dive into the village.

The human streets are narrow, often only supporting at most two humans standing side-by-side. What used to be a positively bustling street is now nothing more than a human lined path as the robots move along the street. No human wants to start an unwinnable conflict with the violent robots — they all squeeze along the sides or slip into the businesses that decorate the road, and they all maintain undying eye-contact with the robots. The two diplomats lead the charge, and right behind are the two doctors. Amongst them, doctor MD-901 is new; This is their first time venturing out of the city and into the village, and soon they will have to apply the medical theory they've learned in simulation to real humans. MD-901 had never even seen humans with their own cameras — each second of footage they record is useful data to look back on once they return into the city. They note everything about the humans from the layers of animal pelt clothing they use to keep warm in this season to the faint aroma of flowers that invades the air. On the shelves of all the buildings packed side-by-side are plant pots of all different shapes, and all populated by fragrant flowers. One of the shops MD-901 passes by, no more than two meters by two meters on the inside, sells distilled fragrances of those very same flowers, spices, and woods. The walls are lined with shelves of fist-sized earthenware pots, and from the roof hangs bunches of dried herbs and leaves. The shop owner sits right in the middle of the cramped space; She takes a break from hawking to observe the passing convoy. Her face scrunches up in disgust as she catches a glimpse of MD-901.

The first step of the protocol is to visit the Town Square where the human diplomats convene with the robot diplomats. As the robots step into this open area, they notice this time around that there are rough squares scratched into the dirt for them to stand in, and so they comply. Two human diplomats warily approach the robot diplomats and slowly extend their hands. These robot diplomats are identical in design, but it would not be easy to come to the conclusion that they are designed for diplomacy with humans. Their form involves many sharp angles and perfectly flat surfaces. They maintain the proportions of a human, but their heads are inharmonious amalgamations of geometry in the general shape of a human head. Their color scheme is cyan upon white, with their names written in black across the side of their abdomens: HD-1013 and HD-1044. They understand the human gesture of hand shaking and reciprocate. The air eases in tension ever so slightly.

"Greetings HD-1044, and HD-1013, my name is Gordon. Thank you for coming today." Gordon says with a warm smile.

HD-1044 responds fluently, "Greetings Gordon. We are here today to once again discuss the terms of your displacement. Due to the results of the last meeting, our leader has unfortunately reduced the amount of aid that we bring today. We hope that we can come to an agreement soon for the benefit of all parties."

Gordon leans over to the side and looks at the supplies that the doctors behind are carrying. Indeed, altogether there is a modest reduction of goods. He turns towards his peer, Wayne, and they nod heads.

In a stern voice Wayne states, "The meeting last week was ... less than ideal. However, we are still firmly sticking to our decision of staying here. The Arturo Border Village has no intention of leaving."

The witnesses all cheer and applaud; The decision is unanimous. HD-1044 looks around and accurately records these humans. With enough evidence, he turns back at Gordon.

"You two put me in a difficult position. May I remind you that we will offer a large compensation in return for your leaving, and we will spare one guard that will join your group for a few days. This is the most generous I've seen V-007."

"We remember Arturo's offer very well, but we will not leave our homes. We built this place with our hands, and Arturo needs to understand that."

HD-1044 stares down at Gordon; Their stature beating Gordon's by a full foot. "Please do not call them Arturo. Our leader's name is V-007."

Foreseeing a prolonged discussion, HD-1013 sends a wireless message to the two doctors, permitting them to begin their rounds. They spin around and leave the town square, but MD-901 is stopped by Wayne who yells out "Hold on!" He scurries over and takes a good look at MD-901. Compared to the other doctor, MD-901 is very different. The other doctor is distinctly artificial; Not as sharp and mechanical as the diplomats, but definitely not designed with an organic influence. MD-901 has a perfect human form, with contours that match that of a grown woman. Wayne looks down at their arms as they clutch at the supply box; Whatever material their made of is deforming under the pressure of the box. Their skin is snow white with a shiny finish along the entire body, save for the extremities which have the same pores and tiny creases that human skin has. MD-901's face is most striking, as it is a perfect replica of a woman's face with gray irises. They are also perfectly bald, revealing a skull shape that is pleasingly human. As Wayne moves closer to their face, they subtly reel back and grimace.

"Fascinating. Why is she different from the other doctors?" Wayne asks.

"V-007 is curious at how a human model will be received. Please let them do their work."

Wayne steps back and takes one good look at MD-901 before returning to Gordon's side. From MD-901's rear-facing camera, they can see many people in the crowd equally intrigued as Wayne glaring right back at them. MD-901 realizes that the attention might hinder their ability to render effective care. The entire layout of the border village is well known and is updated in real-time as any robot passes through the village. The village itself sits right outside the walls of city C-011, which the humans call Arturo City after its leader. This Robo-City, much like every other Robo-City, is enclosed with tall stone walls that prevent any humans from looking inwards. Any human bold enough to scale the wall or somehow sneak in is either publicly killed, or disappeared. Although, that doesn't stop humans from building complex villages right outside the walls. Many have learned that there are actually robots that care for human rights, which led to this strategy of sitting right outside the gates in the hopes of donations and assistance.
